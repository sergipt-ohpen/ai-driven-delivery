# 🧭 How to Use This Guide

## What this is

This guide is a Product Owner / Product Manager adaptation of **AI4Devs 2026/06 Seniors**, a hands-on AI course built for senior developers. The original course teaches engineers how to work with AI assistants like Claude — in the terminal, in the codebase, in the delivery pipeline. We've cut the technical deep-dives (agent configuration, MCP internals, code-level workflows) and kept everything a product person can actually act on: how AI changes planning, how to write backlogs that AI can execute, how to get real work out of Claude every day.

The core insight of the course applies to you more than anyone: **implementation got cheap, so planning became the bottleneck.** A well-defined story now becomes mergeable code in hours. A vague one becomes the wrong code in hours — and the expensive part is a human reviewing and redoing it. The quality of what you write upstream has never mattered more. That's your job. This guide is about doing it with AI, and doing it well.

## Who it's for

Product Owners, Product Managers, and anyone who owns backlogs, PRDs, roadmaps, or stakeholder communication — and who wants to use Claude (claude.ai, Claude Desktop, or Claude Code) without needing to write a line of code. No technical background required. Where a technical concept matters to your work, we explain just enough of it, in plain language.

## What you'll get

- A clear picture of what AI actually does in software delivery today — and where the hype ends.
- A mental model (tool, context, prompt) that explains why AI sometimes works brilliantly and sometimes disappoints.
- Concrete practices: AI-ready backlogs, adversarial PRD reviews, sanity-checked estimates, documentation that both humans and AI can use.
- Ready-to-paste prompts you can use this week.

## How it's organized

- **Why AI changes your job** — the state of AI in delivery, where you fit in an AI-augmented team.
- **Foundations, no jargon** — just enough about how LLMs work to be effective, plus the 2026 assistant landscape.
- **The 3 pillars** — picking the right tool, giving it context, asking well. The framework everything else builds on.
- **Specs: the new shared language** — why spec-driven development matters to product people, and what you contribute.
- **Working with Claude day-to-day** — getting started, what a modern assistant can do, a method that works, and costs & governance.
- **Planning with AI** — your home turf: new planning paradigms, AI-ready backlogs, estimation, PM tools, continuous planning.
- **Documentation & shared knowledge** — closing the documentation deficit, understanding architecture decisions, writing docs that work for humans and AI alike.
- **Your playbook** — 10 things to try with Claude this week, prompts included.

## How to read it

Don't study it — use it. Skim the whole thing once (an hour, maybe less) to build the mental map. Then jump straight to the **playbook** at the end and try two or three of the workflows on your real backlog this week. When something surprises you — a prompt that works better than expected, or worse — come back to the relevant section and read it properly. The guide rewards iteration, not cover-to-cover discipline.

One warning before you start: AI doesn't rescue vague product work. It amplifies it. Everything that follows is about making sure what gets amplified is good.
