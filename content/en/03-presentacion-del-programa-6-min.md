In this section you will understand how AI4Devs really works: not just what you are going to learn, but **how you are going to learn it and with what mindset**.

Our way of teaching is neither theoretical nor passive. It is designed for you to develop a real advantage in your day-to-day work as a developer.

You are not here to "consume content". You are here to **train**.

We work with principles such as *learning by doing*, *learning by teaching*, *Shu-Ha-Ri*, and continuous improvement, because these are what make the difference between someone who understands AI... and someone who uses it with impact.

The goal is not just for you to know how to use AI tools or apply engineering best practices.  
The goal is for you to develop a way of working that is more efficient, more autonomous, and more results-oriented.

If you do the program well, you won't just improve your code.  
It will change the way you approach any technical problem.

## 1\. The Flipped Classroom learning model

At AI4Devs we use the *flipped classroom* model, because it is the one that works best when the goal is learning to build.

The approach is simple:

-   **You learn the concepts on your own**, through structured content (videos, resources, examples).
    
-   **You use the live sessions to apply**, not to listen to theory.
    

This allows us to make the most of our time together on what really adds value: building, making mistakes, iterating, and understanding.

In practice:

-   You arrive at the session with a minimal foundation already worked on.
    
-   During the session, you apply it to real cases.
    
-   The mentor doesn't "explain", but rather guides, corrects, and raises the bar.
    

This turns learning into something active, not passive.

### Why do we work this way?

Because in the real world:

-   Nobody gives you the solution step by step.
    
-   Nobody explains everything before you start.
    
-   And the value lies in knowing how to **solve**, not in knowing how to **repeat**.
    

This model trains you for exactly that.

### What it means for you

This approach requires a bit more commitment, but it also gives you much more in return:

-   Greater autonomy
    
-   Better problem-solving skills
    
-   More speed in learning new tools
    
-   Better technical judgment

## 2\. Language used in the program

Throughout the program we will work primarily with **JavaScript and TypeScript**, both on the backend and the frontend.

This is no coincidence:

-   It is the most widespread ecosystem
    
-   It allows for fast iteration
    
-   It is flexible enough to work with AI and APIs
    

In addition, we will use **GitHub as our base working environment**, since it is part of any developer's day-to-day work.

If you are not familiar with the workflow (*fork + pull request*), here is a clear and practical guide to get started:

👉 [https://lidr.notion.site/C-mo-hacer-tu-primer-Pull-Request-en-GitHub-341a4393278180fe84fae1c4db30048b?source=copy\_link](https://lidr.notion.site/C-mo-hacer-tu-primer-Pull-Request-en-GitHub-341a4393278180fe84fae1c4db30048b?source=copy_link)

In short, this process consists of making a copy (*fork*) of a repository, working on it, and submitting your changes via a *pull request* so they can be reviewed and merged

## 3\. ROTI Feedback

At the end of each session, we will ask you to complete a ROTI (*Return On Time Invested*).

It is not a formality. It is a key tool for improving the program in real time.

It allows us to understand:

-   Whether the session added value
    
-   What worked
    
-   What we need to adjust
    

And it allows us to do so **quickly**, not when it's already too late.

In fact, a large part of the program's improvements come directly from the feedback of students like you
